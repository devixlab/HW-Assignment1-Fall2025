Tyler, The Creator

Tyler, The Creator is an American rapper, singer, and producer known for boundary-pushing music and vision. He is the co-founder of the hip-hop collective Odd Future and recently released his latest album, CHROMAKOPIA.

Early Start

Tyler Gregory Okonma, a.k.a. Tyler, The Creator, was born in 1991 in Ladera Heights, California. He had an early interest in music and started making his first mixtapes and melodies as a teen. In 2007, he co-founded Odd Future, which propelled him to fame as a result of his signature energetic and iterative music style.

Career

Since his debut album "Goblin" in 2011, Tyler has released multiple critically acclaimed albums, winning numerous awards including Grammys for his albums "IGOR" and "Call Me If You Get Lost." His music usually explores themes of identity, creativity, and self-expression, often defying traditional hip-hop composition.
